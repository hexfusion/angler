Friends of the Delaware?

When are the "Friends of the Upper Delaware" not the friends of the river system?
When the "friends" are employing "the sky is falling" public negativity, possibly to such a degree, that the reputation of the Upper Delaware River System is being harmed. When we see the "friends" have only one single demand of 600 cubic feet per second release during the summer, and this only applies to the West Branch. This one demand does not address the way to achieve our long term hydrological needs for the entire river system. Business wise, we certainly would not object to a constant release of 600 CFS which would allow us to do guided float trips all summer, and should produce more consistent fishing. So how do we get to this "ideal" water level from where we are now?

We have no problem with the use of facts made public through the media and even political leverage done in a measured way to make our case. This is how cooperation is achieved. Hard line approaches with unconfirmed negative statements, without scientific fact, made public in the media, may only serve to alienate those parties that have been negotiated with to achieve the current short term gains for the entire system. A quote by Michael Principe, Water Supply Director for New York City makes clear what their position is regarding the 600 CFS release, "Won't happen. There just wouldn't be enough water." Whether this is a true statement or not, would have to be proven. It will be proved true or false via a myriad of studies planned for over the next three years of the current flow agreements. Additionally we have a very deep concern that the negative publicity via the "Friends" will turn off many of those fly fishers who have supported the positive approach taken by the!
Delaware

The approach we favor is to be perceived by all the parties, both pro and con, as being tough but reasonable negotiators working for the long term good of the entire Upper Delaware River System. It is our firm belief that having an even handed approach based on proven fact is the correct and most effective way of achieving these long term goals. We believe this strategy of using factual data, employing political savvy, and working with all other organizations which have the common objective of making the entire upper Delaware River System the best fishery it can be, will indeed work.

This leaves the question of which organization does one support? We recognize that the current minimum flow level agreements are not what we need for the long term. We believe that having a positive attitude with all the parties concerned, using political pressures that are available and continuing with a balanced plan that supports the entire river system are the right steps to eventually get the flows we need. We do not take our stand lightly. Our resort at West Branch Angler has more to lose than anyone. We are the largest outfitter on the Delaware River System with a strong commitment to our guests to provide the best outdoor experience possible. NO ONE has the financial investment, and pays more taxes than we do. With that premise, we have made our decision to give our full support to the Delaware River Foundation. Your support for the Foundation will allow them to continue to make positive progress in protecting the entire Upper Delaware River System for the good of all.

-West Branch Angler
